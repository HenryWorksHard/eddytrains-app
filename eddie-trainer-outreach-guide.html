Eddie's Trainer Recruitment Guide

Getting Beta Testers for CMPD Fitness App · Based on Hunter Dickinson's sales principles

The Offer

Lifetime free access to the CMPD Fitness app in exchange for:

  • Using it with their clients for ~1 month
  • Giving honest feedback on bugs and improvements
  • Potentially providing a testimonial if they love it

Target: 3-6 trainers to start

Who to Target

Ideal profiles:

  • Small to medium fitness creators (1K-50K followers)
  • Personal trainers or online coaches
  • Already posting workout content
  • Have clients they train (not just fitness influencers)
  • Australian preferred (but not required)

Signs they're a good fit:

  • Posts about client transformations
  • Mentions online coaching in bio
  • Uses other fitness apps (shows they're tech-open)
  • Engages with their audience

Avoid: Huge influencers, people who only post gym selfies (no clients), those locked into competitor apps.

The 5 Principles (Hunter Dickinson)

1. Compliment First — Start with genuine appreciation for their content.
"Your transformation posts are sick. That client progress is legit."

2. Give Before You Ask — Lead with what you're offering (free lifetime access).
"We're giving lifetime access to a few trainers to test it out."

3. Peer Group Selling — Make them feel part of an exclusive early group.
"We're only taking 3-6 trainers for this first round."

4. Make Them Feel Special — Explain why you reached out to THEM.
"Saw your content and thought you'd be perfect for this."

5. No Pressure Positioning — It's free, no commitment.
"No pressure at all — just seeing if you'd be keen to test it."

What You're Offering

The app does:

  • Programs with workouts, sets, reps, rest timers
  • Clients can log their weights and track progress
  • Calendar view of scheduled workouts
  • Trainer dashboard to manage multiple clients

What's in it for them:

  • Free tool to manage clients professionally
  • Input on features (we'll actually build what they need)
  • Early adopter status ("founding trainer")

The Conversation Framework

1. Open with a Genuine Compliment

"Hey man, been seeing your content. That client transformation you posted last week was insane."

2. Find Out About Their Setup

"Quick question — what do you use to send programs to your clients?"

Listen for pain points: Google Sheets, Excel/PDFs, expensive apps, nothing.

3. Plant the Seed

"So my mate's been building this fitness app for trainers — lets you send programs, clients log their weights, track progress. He's looking for a few trainers to test it out. Lifetime free access in exchange for using it with some clients and giving feedback."

4. Make It Exclusive

"Only taking like 3-6 trainers for this first round. Thought of you because your content's legit and you actually train real clients."

5. The Ask (No Pressure)

"No pressure at all — just seeing if you'd be keen to check it out? Can send you a link to have a look."

Handling Responses

"Yeah sounds interesting"
→ "Sweet, it's app.cmpdcollective.com — sign up as a trainer and have a play. Let me know what you think."

"What's the catch?"
→ "No catch. It's new, so we need real trainers to use it and tell us what's missing. You get free lifetime access, we get feedback. Win-win."

"I already use [competitor]"
→ "Yeah fair. This one's still early but might be worth a look — especially if you're paying for the other one. This is free forever for early testers."

"I don't have many clients"
→ "All good, even just testing with one or two would be heaps helpful."

"Not interested"
→ "No worries at all man. Keep killing it with the content."

After They Sign Up

  1. Welcome: "Sick, you're in! Have a play around, add a client or two."
  2. Check in (1 week): "How's the app going? Any bugs or stuff that's confusing?"
  3. Collect feedback: Write down everything — bugs, feature requests, confusion points.
  4. After ~1 month: "Would you be open to giving us a quick testimonial?"

Key Messages

  • Lifetime free access — real value, not a trial
  • Only 3-6 spots — exclusive, not mass outreach
  • We actually listen — their feedback shapes the app
  • No pressure — they can say no
  • Made for real trainers — not generic fitness app

What NOT to Do

❌ Don't spam — quality over quantity

❌ Don't oversell — it's free, let that speak

❌ Don't be pushy — you're offering value

❌ Don't follow up more than twice

❌ Don't promise features that don't exist

❌ Don't target huge influencers
