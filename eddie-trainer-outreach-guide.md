# Eddie's Trainer Recruitment Guide
## Getting Beta Testers for CMPD Fitness App

*Based on Hunter Dickinson's sales principles*

---

## The Offer

**Lifetime free access** to the CMPD Fitness app in exchange for:
- Using it with their clients for ~1 month
- Giving honest feedback on bugs and improvements
- Potentially providing a testimonial if they love it

**Target:** 3-6 trainers to start

---

## Who to Target

**Ideal profiles:**
- Small to medium fitness creators (1K-50K followers)
- Personal trainers or online coaches
- Already posting workout content
- Have clients they train (not just fitness influencers)
- Australian preferred (but not required)

**Signs they're a good fit:**
- Posts about client transformations
- Mentions online coaching in bio
- Uses other fitness apps (shows they're tech-open)
- Engages with their audience (not just posting)

**Avoid:**
- Huge influencers (won't care about a new app)
- People who only post gym selfies (no clients)
- Already locked into competitor apps

---

## The 5 Principles (Hunter Dickinson)

### 1. Compliment First
Start with genuine appreciation for their content.

*"Your transformation posts are sick. That client progress is legit."*

### 2. Give Before You Ask
You're offering them something valuable (free lifetime access) — lead with that.

*"We're giving lifetime access to a few trainers to test it out."*

### 3. Peer Group Selling
Make them feel part of an exclusive early group.

*"We're only taking 3-6 trainers for this first round."*

### 4. Make Them Feel Special
Explain why you reached out to THEM specifically.

*"Saw your content and thought you'd be perfect for this."*

### 5. No Pressure Positioning
It's free, no commitment, just try it.

*"No pressure at all — just seeing if you'd be keen to test it."*

## What You're Offering

**The app does:**
- Programs with workouts, sets, reps, rest timers
- Clients can log their weights and track progress
- Calendar view of scheduled workouts
- Trainer dashboard to manage multiple clients
- Works on phone and desktop

**The deal:**
- Lifetime free access (normally will be paid)
- They use it with a few clients for about a month
- They tell us what's good, what's broken, what's missing
- If they love it, we'd ask for a testimonial later

**What's in it for them:**
- Free tool to manage clients professionally
- Input on features (we'll actually build what they need)
- Early adopter status (can say they were "founding trainers")

## The Conversation Framework

### 1. Open with a Genuine Compliment
Check their profile first. Comment on something specific.

*"Hey man, been seeing your content. That client transformation you posted last week was insane."*

*"Yo your coaching content is solid. How long you been training people?"*

### 2. Find Out About Their Setup
Ask about how they currently manage clients.

*"Quick question — what do you use to send programs to your clients?"*

*"You got an app or just send spreadsheets/PDFs?"*

**Listen for pain points:**
- "Just use Google Sheets" → manual, messy
- "Excel/PDF" → hard for clients to use
- "An app but it's expensive" → cost issue
- "Nothing really" → needs a solution

### 3. Plant the Seed
Once you know their situation, introduce the app naturally.

*"Ah nice. So my mate's been building this fitness app for trainers — lets you send programs, clients log their weights, track progress, all that. Pretty clean actually."*

*"He's looking for a few trainers to test it out and give feedback. Lifetime free access in exchange for using it with some clients and telling us what works and what doesn't."*

### 4. Make It Exclusive
Create urgency through scarcity (but keep it chill).

*"Only taking like 3-6 trainers for this first round. Thought of you because your content's legit and you actually train real clients."*

### 5. The Ask (No Pressure)
Make it easy to say yes.

*"No pressure at all — just seeing if you'd be keen to check it out? Can send you a link to have a look."*

*"If it's not your thing, all good. But figured I'd reach out since you'd actually use it properly."*

## Handling Responses

**"Yeah sounds interesting, send it through"**
→ *"Sweet, it's app.cmpdcollective.com — sign up as a trainer and have a play. Let me know what you think."*
→ Follow up in 2-3 days: *"Did you get a chance to check it out?"*

**"What's the catch?"**
→ *"No catch honestly. It's new, so we need real trainers to use it and tell us what's missing. You get free lifetime access, we get feedback. Win-win."*

**"I already use [competitor app]"**
→ *"Yeah fair. This one's still early but might be worth a look — especially if you're paying for the other one. This is free forever for early testers."*

**"How does it work?"**
→ *"You create programs, assign them to clients, they see their workouts on their phone and log what they lift. You can see their progress from your dashboard. Pretty simple."*

**"I don't have many clients right now"**
→ *"All good, even just testing it with one or two would be heaps helpful. Doesn't need to be a big thing."*

**"Not interested"**
→ *"No worries at all man. Keep killing it with the content."* (Stay friendly, maybe they'll come back later)

**"Let me think about it"**
→ *"Yeah of course. Just know spots are limited so if you're keen, sooner is better. No stress though."*

## Follow-Up Schedule

| Situation | Wait | Action |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Sent link, no response | 3 days | *"Hey did you get a chance to check the app out?"* |
| Still nothing | 7 days | *"All good if you're busy. Offer's there if you want it."* |
| After that | — | Move on. Don't chase further. |

**Rule:** Maximum 2 follow-ups, then let it go.

---

## After They Sign Up

Once someone creates a trainer account:

1. **Welcome them:** *"Sick, you're in! Have a play around, add a client or two, and let us know what you think."*

2. **Check in after a week:** *"How's the app going? Any bugs or stuff that's confusing?"*

3. **Collect feedback:** Write down everything they say — bugs, feature requests, confusion points.

4. **After ~1 month:** *"Hey appreciate you testing this out. Would you be open to giving us a quick testimonial if you've found it useful?"*

---

## Key Messages to Land

1. **Lifetime free access** — real value, not a trial
2. **Only 3-6 spots** — exclusive, not mass outreach
3. **We actually listen** — their feedback shapes the app
4. **No pressure** — they can say no and it's all good
5. **Made for real trainers** — not just another generic fitness app

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Don't spam — quality over quantity
❌ Don't oversell — it's free, let that speak for itself
❌ Don't be pushy — you're offering value, not begging
❌ Don't follow up more than twice — respect their time
❌ Don't promise features that don't exist — be honest about where it's at
❌ Don't target huge influencers — they won't care
